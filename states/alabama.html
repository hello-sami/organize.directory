Statewide Initiatives

Alabama Justice Initiative

A grassroots organization working to end mass incarceration in Alabama through mutual aid, community organizing, and advocacy. They provide direct support to incarcerated individuals and their families while building community power for systemic change.

Justice for Lakeith Smith

A mutual aid campaign providing support and resources while advocating for justice for Lakeith Smith and other youth affected by Alabama's accomplice liability law.

Hometown Action

A multi-racial, working-class organization building mutual aid networks and community power in small-town and rural Alabama communities, with a focus on racial, gender, economic, and climate justice.

Alabama Queer Resistance

A mutual aid collective providing support, resources, and community organizing for LGBTQ+ individuals across Alabama, with a focus on direct support and building queer community power.

¡HICA! (Hispanic Interest Coalition of Alabama)

A community development and advocacy organization that champions economic equality, civic engagement, and social justice for Latino and immigrant families in Alabama through mutual aid, direct support services, and community empowerment programs.

Alabama Books to Prisons Project

A mutual aid initiative providing books and educational materials to incarcerated individuals across Alabama, while also facilitating penpal connections and publishing an Inside Out Newsletter. They work to reduce isolation in prison life and increase access to educational resources.

Communities Not Prisons

A coalition of directly impacted people, communities, and organizations working to stop prison construction in Alabama through mutual aid, community organizing, and advocacy. They focus on supporting communities affected by mass incarceration while envisioning alternatives to prison expansion.
