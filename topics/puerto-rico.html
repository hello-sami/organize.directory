Puerto Rico Solidarity Initiatives

7 groups

  1. Brigada Solidaria del Oeste ↗ brigada.org

    A community-based mutual aid organization that emerged after Hurricane Maria to fill gaps in disaster response. They coordinate direct aid, distribute supplies, and organize volunteers for community rebuilding efforts across western Puerto Rico.

  2. Proyecto Agua Sol ↗ aguasol.org

    A community-led initiative working to install solar-powered water pumps and filtration systems in rural communities across Puerto Rico, ensuring access to clean water during disasters and power outages.

  3. Casa Pueblo ↗ casapueblo.org

    A community-based organization promoting energy autonomy through solar power and sustainable practices. They've created a community-owned solar electric system and continue to advocate for Puerto Rico's energy independence and environmental protection.

  4. Boricua Sanctuary Movement ↗ boricuasanctuary.org

    A network connecting Puerto Rican diaspora communities with on-island organizers to coordinate solidarity efforts, mutual aid, and political advocacy campaigns fighting for Puerto Rico's self-determination and against austerity measures.

  5. PROMESA Impact Project ↗ promesaimpactproject.org

    A coalition documenting and challenging the impacts of the PROMESA fiscal control board and austerity policies. They provide legal resources, community education, and organize resistance to displacement and privatization efforts.

  6. Ayuda Legal Puerto Rico ↗ ayudalegalpr.org

    A legal aid organization providing free legal assistance to communities affected by disasters, displacement, and housing insecurity. They focus on community education, legal clinics, and policy advocacy for equitable disaster recovery and housing justice.

  7. JUPI USA ↗ instagram.com

    A social justice organization focused on advocating for Puerto Rico's rights and supporting solidarity initiatives. They share resources, organize community events, and raise awareness about issues affecting Puerto Ricans both on the island and in the diaspora.
