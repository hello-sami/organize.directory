Southwest Corridor Equity Coalition (SWEC)

Coalition of community organizations fighting displacement and advocating for equitable development in Southwest Portland through grassroots organizing, mutual aid, and community-led planning initiatives.

350PDX

Grassroots climate justice organization building people power to tackle the root causes of climate chaos through mutual aid, direct action, and solidarity with frontline communities.

OPAL Environmental Justice Oregon

A grassroots organization building power for environmental justice and civil rights in low-income communities and communities of color through organizing, leadership development, and mutual aid. Their work focuses on transit justice, housing rights, and climate action while centering the voices of those most impacted by environmental racism.

Revolution Rising PDX

Grassroots collective building mutual aid networks and community power through direct action, political education, and solidarity organizing.

Social Justice Action Center (SJAC)

A community organizing space and mutual aid hub providing resources and support for grassroots movements and social justice initiatives in Portland. SJAC serves as a gathering place for organizing, skill-sharing, and building collective power.

The People's Store PDX

Mobile mutual aid resourcing unhoused people on occupied Multnomah, Cowlitz, & Grand Ronde land.

Don't Shoot PDX

Black-led social justice organization focused on art, education, and mutual aid initiatives. Provides community resources through their Black Culture Library, art exhibitions, and direct action programs while advocating for civil rights and racial justice.

SURJ PDX

A chapter of Showing Up for Racial Justice fostering mutual aid and community solidarity through white anti-racist organizing in the Portland Metropolitan Area. Founded in 2013, they coordinate political education, direct action support, and solidarity networks while building power through racial justice education and accountable organizing in partnership with BIPOC-led organizations. Their work is guided by values including calling people in rather than calling out, taking risks and learning from mistakes, and organizing out of mutual interest.

Black Food Sovereignty Coalition

Coalition igniting Black and Brown communities to build food sovereignty, community wealth, and leadership within food systems. Operates programs including Come Thru Market, Grandma's Hands, and Back To The Root while supporting Black-owned farms and food initiatives.

Organization building leadership, healing, and safety for Black-African transgender, queer, nonbinary, two-spirit, and intersex (TQN2SI+) Oregonians through community support and mutual aid initiatives.

Anti-Binary PDX

Mutual aid collective providing direct support and resources to trans and nonbinary community members in Portland through solidarity networks and community care.
