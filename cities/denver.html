Community Organizing

Movimiento Poder

A grassroots organization building power in immigrant, Chicano, and Indigenous communities through mutual aid, leadership development, and organizing for racial, economic, and climate justice. Their work includes youth organizing, immigration rights advocacy, and community-led campaigns for systemic change.

Denver Communists

A grassroots organization fostering mutual aid and community solidarity through socialist organizing in Denver. They coordinate direct support, political education, and solidarity networks while building power through community organizing and creating spaces for collective action and community care.

Food Security

SAME Cafe

A non-profit cafe fostering mutual aid and food justice through pay-what-you-can meals in Denver. They coordinate food access, volunteer programs, and job training while building power through sustainable food systems, community nourishment, and creating spaces for dignity and inclusion in food service.

Denver Community Fridges

Operates 5 community fridges in the Denver Metro area.

Racial Justice

Denver Black Reparations Council

A community organization advocating for and implementing reparations for Black communities in Denver through mutual aid, policy advocacy, and community organizing. They work to address historical and ongoing racial inequities through direct support, education, and building pathways for racial and economic justice.

Reparations Circle Denver

A community of contributors working to build reparative change through direct funding and support of Black-led initiatives in Denver. They partner with the Denver Black Reparations Council to coordinate grant-making, mutual aid, and community building while fostering a culture of repair and racial healing through education, dialogue, and collective action.

Radical Publishing

Liberating Library

A digital library and mutual aid resource fostering radical education and knowledge sharing in Denver. They provide free access to a comprehensive collection of texts on topics including African diaspora studies, anarchism, environmentalism, gender and queer studies, indigenous perspectives, labor history, and revolutionary movements while building power through accessible education and community learning.

Little Read Books

A community bookstore and mutual aid space fostering literacy and radical education in Denver. They coordinate book access, reading programs, and solidarity networks while building power through accessible literature, community learning, and creating spaces for literary engagement and collective knowledge sharing.
