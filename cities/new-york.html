New York City

Community Safety & Support

We Build The Block

A grassroots organization coordinating mutual aid and community-led public safety initiatives in New York City. They organize youth leadership development, restorative justice programs, and innovative safety protocols while building networks of solidarity through community education, trauma resolution, and transformative approaches to neighborhood wellbeing.

Prison Support & Reentry

Parole Preparation Project

A grassroots organization fostering mutual aid and community solidarity through advocacy, litigation, and direct support for currently and formerly incarcerated New Yorkers. They coordinate parole preparation assistance, community care programs, and solidarity networks while building power through policy advocacy, arts programming, and creating spaces for successful reentry and community reintegration.

Education & Culture

The Free Black Women's Library

A mobile library and mutual aid initiative celebrating Black women authors and literature. Through book exchanges, cultural events, and community gatherings, they create accessible spaces for learning, sharing resources, and building connections while centering Black women's voices and experiences.

Common Notions

A radical publishing collective fostering mutual aid through accessible political education and movement literature. They work to amplify marginalized voices, support social movements, and create resources for community organizing while building networks of knowledge sharing and solidarity.

AJ Muste Institute

A foundation supporting grassroots activism and mutual aid through grants, fiscal sponsorship, and community space. They provide resources for social justice organizing, nonviolent action, and movement building while fostering connections between activists and organizations working for transformative change.

Food Justice

Food Not Bombs Lower East Side

A mutual aid collective fostering food justice and community solidarity through free food sharing in New York City's Lower East Side. They coordinate regular meal distributions, food recovery initiatives, and solidarity networks while building power through direct action, community care, and creating spaces for food sovereignty and collective nourishment.
